# counter

mu/nu is ... hard to arrive at from inside the nu layer

counter is an app (literally a CRUDy point-and-click adventure) that meets the grain of your phenomenology, deriving your personal interfaces, physics, and thermodynamic terrain as you go. "we're all walking each other home" okay yeah but this is about specifically locating your home address, in your own native address-space, the one that maybe nobody else you've ever met would recognize

counter (n): a clicky thing, increments by one. counter is a simple incrementer, unsimple only in that it lets you narrow down the type(s) of thing you're counting as you go, but the first click is always unitless, it's just *click*

counter (v): rotation into the imaginary plane. compare: *block*

count (v): you count, it counts, pun intentional

usecounter.com (it counts uses, it's an invitation to *use* the thing called *counter*, it counts your *you's*, it's an aikido translator, whatever works for you)

you wake up *mid-games*, as in you're in the middle of an unknown set of games-already-in-progress. counter is for solving for getting those games landed. all of them. once they're all landed, life becomes creative mode. :) (see "creative mode" mentioned in 3-perspectives/*.md)

important: in the way that good storytelling unexpectedly wraps several threads at once, the Counter user experience (if we've got the design right) will regularly offer clean exits, residue-free. not merely "no strings attached", more like "if you wanna drop that particular string here's a place to anchor your end of it that isn't you"

## the cursor (`Counter/Cursor.lean`)

the gui expedition's first carve: the screen is a zipper. a page is blank or a click atop kids-and-sibs — a rose forest compiled left-child/right-sibling into a binary tree, and that compilation is *why* the toolbox holds exactly three tools (ascend, enter, advance; `the_toolbox_holds_three`, each held once by the Ledger root's own freq). affordance is local — the ascent reads the trail, the entrance reads the focus — so what you can do is a fact about where you sit, never about the far page. every use that shaves something off lists the loss *with its type* in the crumb (`the_entrance_lists_the_loss`), and the loss is recoverable: every entrance writes its exit — the constitutive property, surfacing at the drawing layer. the census splits at the seat (focus + trail = page, conserved under every use, 100% at entry), the exit home is already written and costs exactly the trail, the page seats its census (as many seats as clicks: hollow, inhabitable), and every seat relays the whole. the closing law: `the_walks_read_exactly_the_page` — a walk in the three tools connects two seats iff they hold the same page. reachability *is* page-equality; you can never leave, and you can always arrive. more tools never need handing out — you've just gotta move to the pov from which the composition is one step.

## the hand (`Counter/Hand.lean`)

the toolbox, dealt: at every seat the three slots hold their live cards — each card an `Option Cursor`, the destination or nothing, specifically-typed. no card holds its own seat (every use moves), and every use mints a card the spent hand never held: the return, the specifically-typed undo, created *by* the move — tool creation once per move, which is how moves already work everywhere; Counter just makes it discoverable. the hand cannot name its holder: the two vacancies flanking a bare click hold byte-identical hands over the same page (face-blindness at the card level — one hand between two seats 🎴), and that ambiguity lives exactly at the vacancies, nowhere else. the blank page deals no card at all and provably cannot move itself: the first click is an interruption — Counter opens by being met, never by moving (the weld to `Change.lean`'s membrane, where the arrival is the noticing). and the regress grounds twice over: no page swallows itself (the census strictly decreases inward), the whole shows on the focus only at the entry, yet every interior seat still relays the whole — so a model of Counter inside Counter is always a *seat*, never a copy, and its collapse is the relay. the strange loop grounds.

## the dive (`Counter/Dive.lean`)

the mode boundary between designing the apparatus and inhabiting it, typed. `Page.entry` is enter-the-simulation, `Cursor.page` is surfacing: the dive is lossless (`page ∘ entry = id` — the model survives), the surfacing forgets the seat (the entry seats are exactly the fixed points; the price of the view-from-everywhere is being nowhere in particular). the view-from-everywhere itself is just holding the `∀`: flow rates are the census splits, pressure points are the seats where the hand narrows, and every walk is priceable without walking it. and the load-bearing no: the seat register is a tree — every return is even, and a windless walk never returns — sealed against the toll booth in one coin: the wheel winds where the tree cannot (four identical clicks return every spin; four identical enters return no seat). clicks are the odometer of use: charged per move at the gross register, felt as net displacement — the keeper/wheel complementarity, arriving at the drawing table.
